Hail Damage: More Than Just Cosmetic

Many drivers assume hail damage is purely a cosmetic issue, but the reality is more serious than most people realize. When hailstones strike your vehicle at high speed, they create concentrated impact points that affect far more than the surface appearance.

Paint and clear coat damage. Even when a hail dent looks minor, the force of the impact can crack the clear coat or fracture the paint layer underneath. These microscopic cracks may not be visible right away, but they allow moisture to reach the bare metal. Over time, that moisture causes oxidation and rust that spreads beneath the paint surface, leading to far more expensive repairs down the road.

Structural panel weakening. Repeated hail impacts across a single panel can weaken the metal, reducing its ability to perform as designed in a future collision. Hood and roof panels, in particular, play a role in distributing crash forces. Leaving dozens or hundreds of dents unrepaired can compromise that structural performance.

Resale and trade-in value. Unrepaired hail damage is one of the fastest ways to reduce your vehicle's market value. Dealerships and private buyers heavily discount hail-damaged vehicles, and the loss in value almost always exceeds the cost of repair, especially when insurance covers the work.

Glass damage. Large hailstones frequently crack or chip windshields, side windows, and sunroofs. A compromised windshield is a safety hazard: it contributes to the structural rigidity of the cabin and supports proper airbag deployment. Our glass repair and replacement services ensure your vehicle's safety systems remain intact.

PDR: The Preferred Hail Damage Solution

Paintless dent repair, commonly known as PDR, has become the industry-standard method for repairing hail damage when the paint surface remains intact. Rather than sanding, filling, and repainting each dented panel, PDR technicians use specialized metal rods and tools to carefully massage the dented metal back to its original shape from behind the panel.

Preserves the factory finish. Because PDR does not involve any sanding or repainting, your vehicle retains its original factory paint. This is significant for both appearance and value. Original paint is more durable than aftermarket refinishing, and vehicles with factory paint consistently command higher resale prices.

Faster turnaround. Traditional hail repair involving body filler, primer, paint, and clear coat can take one to two weeks or longer depending on the severity. PDR repairs for moderate hail damage are often completed in one to three days, getting you back on the road much sooner.

Lower cost. Without the expense of paint materials, booth time, and the labor-intensive sanding and refinishing process, PDR is significantly less expensive than conventional body repair. This often means a lower insurance claim, a smaller impact on your premium history, and in some cases, a repair cost that falls below your deductible.

Environmentally friendly. PDR eliminates the use of body filler, primer, solvent-based paints, and clear coat. This makes it a greener repair option with no volatile organic compound (VOC) emissions and no material waste.

When Hail Damage Requires Traditional Repair

While PDR is ideal for the majority of hail damage, some situations call for conventional body repair methods. Our technicians assess every vehicle individually and recommend the approach that delivers the best result for your specific damage.

Cracked or chipped paint. When hail impacts are severe enough to crack the paint or clear coat, PDR alone cannot restore the surface. In these cases, the affected panels require sanding, priming, and refinishing with our computerized paint matching technology to achieve a seamless, factory-quality finish.

Sharp or creased dents. Hailstones that create sharp creases or very deep dents may stretch the metal beyond what PDR can reverse. These dents often require body filler, reshaping, and repainting to restore the panel to its original contour.

Broken glass. Hail frequently damages windshields, rear windows, and sunroof glass. We provide complete glass replacement with proper ADAS recalibration to ensure your vehicle's advanced safety systems continue to function correctly after the new glass is installed.

Damaged trim, moldings, and accessories. Large hail can crack mirror housings, dent roof rails, and damage antenna masts or other exterior components. We source OEM and OEM-equivalent replacement parts to restore every detail of your vehicle's appearance.

Filing an Insurance Claim for Hail Damage

Hail damage is covered under the comprehensive portion of your auto insurance policy, which is separate from your collision coverage. If you carry comprehensive insurance, your policy will cover hail repairs minus your deductible. Here is what you need to know about the claims process.

Document the damage. After a hail storm, photograph your vehicle from multiple angles in good lighting. Capture close-up shots of individual dents and wider shots showing the overall extent of the damage. These photos support your claim and provide a clear record of the storm's impact.

Contact your insurance company. Report the hail damage to your insurer as soon as possible. Most companies have specific time limits for filing weather-related claims. Your adjuster will schedule an inspection or may accept a shop estimate directly.

Understand your deductible. Your comprehensive deductible applies to hail claims. Deductibles typically range from $250 to $1,000. If the repair cost is close to or below your deductible, you may choose to pay out of pocket to avoid a claim on your record.

Indiana Hail Season: What Lafayette, Indiana Drivers Should Know

Indiana's severe weather season typically runs from late March through early September, with the highest frequency of damaging hail occurring in May, June, and July. Tippecanoe County, Indiana sits in the heart of the Midwest's storm corridor, where warm, moist air from the Gulf of Mexico collides with cooler air masses moving through the Great Plains. This geographic position makes Lafayette and the surrounding region particularly vulnerable to supercell thunderstorms that produce large hail.

Hailstone size matters. The National Weather Service issues severe thunderstorm warnings when hail of one inch in diameter or larger is expected. However, even quarter-inch hailstones can dent vehicle panels and crack windshields when driven by high winds. Storms in central Indiana have historically produced hailstones exceeding two inches in diameter, large enough to shatter glass and cause thousands of dollars in damage to a single vehicle.

Preparation tips for Lafayette drivers. Park in a garage or covered structure whenever severe weather is forecast. If no covered parking is available, use a padded car cover designed for hail protection. Monitor the National Weather Service and local forecasts during storm season, and move your vehicle to shelter when warnings are issued. After any storm that produces hail, inspect your vehicle carefully in daylight and run your hand across panels to feel for dents that may not be immediately visible.

When a major hail event hits Tippecanoe County, Indiana, repair shops can become overwhelmed with demand. Scheduling your repair promptly at Bob Rohrman Collision Repair Center ensures you get on the schedule quickly and avoids the longer wait times that develop in the weeks following a widespread storm.

Frequently Asked Questions About Hail Damage Repair

Does insurance cover hail damage repair?

Yes. Hail damage is typically covered under the comprehensive portion of your auto insurance policy. You will be responsible for your deductible, but the remainder of the repair cost is covered by your insurer. Bob Rohrman Collision Repair Center works directly with all major insurance companies to streamline the claims process. Learn more about our insurance claims assistance.

How long does hail damage repair take?

Repair time depends on the severity of the damage. Minor hail damage repaired with paintless dent repair (PDR) can often be completed in one to three days. More severe damage requiring traditional bodywork, paint, or glass replacement may take one to two weeks. We provide a detailed timeline during your free estimate.

What is paintless dent repair and is it good for hail damage?

Paintless dent repair (PDR) is a technique that removes dents from the vehicle body without sanding, filling, or repainting. Specialized tools are used to gently push the metal back to its original shape from behind the panel. PDR is the preferred method for hail damage because it preserves the factory paint finish, costs less than traditional repair, and can be completed much faster.

Should I get hail damage repaired even if it looks minor?

Yes. Even small hail dents can crack the clear coat or compromise the paint, exposing bare metal to moisture and leading to rust over time. Unrepaired hail damage also significantly reduces your vehicle's resale value. Addressing the damage promptly protects both the structural integrity and the value of your car.
